## 3) THE PLAYBOOK — how small creators/studios actually landed CPG deals

Three patterns that work, with real precedent:

### Example 1 — Bang Energy's open-UGC model (the "make it first, tag us" door)
Bang Energy built ~$300M in sales partly by giving creators near-total freedom: produce whatever you
want as long as a can is in-frame and you use the brand hashtag. They onboarded a huge ambassador
network that churns out dozens of videos/month for **visibility**, not polish.
**Lesson for us:** energy brands reward *volume + presence* over big-agency polish. Make spec content
WITH their product visible, tag them, and use it as the proof you can produce at volume — then convert
the relationship into a paid content-partnership.
(Source: Extole case study.)

### Example 2 — The spec-reel / "fake ad that got real" cold pitch
The repeatable creator playbook (documented across UGC creators and agencies): you do **not** need
100k followers — brands hire on **creator-product fit and proof of work**, not audience size. The move
that converts: cut an **unsolicited spec ad** for the exact brand (their can, their colors, your
cinematic edit + original track), then send it cold to the social/influencer manager with one line:
*"Made this for you on spec — want 4 more this week?"* Authentic, fast content beats polished agency
decks; brands increasingly prefer it. UGC creators routinely land first deals this way via cold pitch
to the right marketing inbox (not support).
**Lesson for us:** our entire edge — fast, cheap, cinematic, original music — is *built* for the spec-ad
cold open. Ship the artifact first; the artifact IS the pitch.
(Sources: Hummingbirds on creator-product fit; Whop / UGC-creator playbooks; Quimby Digital.)

### Example 3 — The "billion-dollar beverage" challenger studios (OLIPOP / poppi / Liquid Death)
These brands won by treating **content as the product** — entertainment-first, internet-native creative
produced fast and cheap relative to legacy TV. Liquid Death especially built an in-house/creator content
engine and a "creator network" rather than relying on one big agency; poppi scaled on TikTok-native,
shoppable, high-volume creative before PepsiCo. Small studios/creators got in early by **matching the
brand's voice and delivering finished assets**, not pitching strategy.
**Lesson for us:** approach challenger/mid-size brands (Section 2) as a *content engine* — "we'll feed
your feed weekly with cinematic shoppable assets + original BGM" — which is exactly the OLIPOP/poppi/LD
operating model, just AI-accelerated.
(Sources: Propeller "Billion-Dollar Beverage Blueprint"; Marketing Dive on poppi; new/day studio case studies.)
