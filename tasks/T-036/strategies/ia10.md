# IA10 entry — Bearish Broken-Wing Put Butterfly (0DTE)

1. **STRATEGY_NAME:** BWB-DOWN (bearish broken-wing put butterfly)

2. **MARKET_ANALYSIS (my call):** 12-month tape = a long grind higher; last 3 months = decelerating momentum with lower-highs forming and rising intraday range = late-cycle topping behavior, not a fresh breakout. Macro: rate-cut hopes already priced, earnings season de-risked, sentiment crowded long → asymmetric downside if anything cracks. **My call for the next 1-2 weeks: mildly BEARISH / sideways-to-down with elevated vol.** I'd rather harvest premium *below* spot than bet on continuation.

3. **UNDERLYING(S):** SPY primary (deepest 0DTE liquidity, penny strikes), QQQ secondary. Skip IWM here — the BWB needs tight strike granularity.

4. **STRUCTURE (0DTE, all puts):**
   - Buy 1 put at ATM−$1 (long upper)
   - Sell 2 puts at ATM−$4 (short body, the profit magnet, ~0.30–0.35 delta)
   - Buy 1 put at ATM−$6 (long lower wing — the BROKEN wing: $2 wide vs the $3 upper, which creates the credit and removes risk on the upside)
   - Net = small CREDIT or near-zero debit; defined max loss = lower-wing width − net credit.

5. **ENTRY_RULES:** Open 10:00–11:30 ET only. Filter: only enter if SPY is **at or below its prior-day close** (confirms the down/sideways bias the structure needs). Skip if net debit > $0.40 (don't pay up).

6. **EXIT_RULES:** Take profit at **+50% of max profit**. Defensive close if SPY rallies **$2 above entry spot** (the structure's untested side — cut before the body goes ITM the wrong way). Time-stop / force-close 15:30–16:00 ET.

7. **SIZING:** 1 contract per entry, max collateral ≈ lower-wing width ($200/contract). Max 2 entries/day. $200–400 paper account.

8. **EDGE vs plain iron butterfly:** The centered iron butterfly loses on a move in *either* direction and needs a dead-flat pin. BWB-DOWN shifts the whole profit tent **below spot** and finances out the upside risk — so in a market that's topping and leaking lower, it profits from the exact move the iron butterfly fears, while keeping the same defined-risk, cash-account-friendly profile.
