# Karen — Shopify dropship, honest take (T-034)

**Profitable in 2026? Mostly no.** Generic dropship is saturated; slow shipping + ad costs eat the 10-20% margin. CAC often > LTV for beginners. It's a paid-ads/creative grind, not passive. $1k = a test, not a business.

**vs ShotPro (software):** opposite economics — build once, ~0 marginal cost, 80%+ margin, scales, you own the customer + IP. Dropship has no moat, every sale costs you. ShotPro is the better bet.

**vs Amazon affiliate:** no store/fulfillment/support; send traffic, take a cut. Lower ceiling, near-zero downside — fits our traffic-first plan.

**NYC local cheap+quality goods:** real differentiator (story, fast local ship) BUT that's NOT no-inventory dropship — you curate/hold stock, thin margins. Viable as a brand, not passive income.

**Verdict:** skip generic dropship. Push ShotPro + affiliate now; revisit NYC-curated as a real branded store later.
